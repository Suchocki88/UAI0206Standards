Understanding AI: A Conceptual Approach
Chapter 2, Lesson 2.6: Building the Core of Your Project (Creative Project Day)

Lesson 2.6 is a Creative Project Day where students apply insights from Lessons 2.3-2.5 about AI capabilities to develop foundational elements of their semester-long creative projects through strategic AI collaboration. Students work on characters, voice, themes, or other core elements while maintaining creative control and documenting where AI collaboration succeeds and struggles. This lesson integrates creative writing, technology literacy, critical evaluation, and metacognitive reflection.

Crosscutting Concepts

Patterns
Different patterns may be observed at each of the scales at which a system is studied and can provide evidence for causality in explanations of phenomena.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Students observe patterns in AI responses: tendency toward common archetypes, improved quality with structured prompts, generic suggestions when requests are vague, struggles with authentic emotional depth—patterns providing evidence for how training data shapes AI's creative capabilities.
Cause and Effect: Mechanism and Explanation
Cause and effect relationships may be used to predict phenomena in natural or designed systems.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Students identify cause-effect relationships through experimentation: structured prompts (cause) ? more specific responses (effect); vague requests (cause) ? generic outputs (effect); specific creative direction (cause) ? AI follows rather than leads (effect). Understanding these mechanisms helps predict AI behavior in future creative work.
Systems and System Models
Models can be used to predict the behavior of a system. Systems may interact with other systems; they may have sub-systems and be a part of larger complex systems.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Students develop mental models of AI creative collaboration: AI system (trained patterns + prompt processing) interacts with human system (evaluation + selection), creating larger creative system where AI suggests and humans decide—model predicting that clearer inputs and critical evaluation yield better creative outcomes.
Structure and Function
The way in which an object or living organism is shaped and its substructure determine many of its properties and functions.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Students discover how AI system structure (trained on text patterns, uses pattern matching without understanding) determines its creative functions and limitations: good at generating options based on common patterns, struggles with authentic emotional depth, requires human judgment for genuine creativity.

Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Through experiencing AI collaboration, students understand economic incentives and policy implications: Companies are incentivized to deploy creative AI (reduces costs, speeds production) with benefits for some (access to creative assistance) but potential costs for others (creative workers facing automation, users receiving generic content).

Dimension 3: Evaluating Sources and Using Evidence

D3.1.9-12
Gather relevant information from multiple sources representing a wide range of views while using the origin, authority, structure, context, and corroboration to guide the selection.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Students gather information from multiple "sources" (different AI prompting approaches, different creative challenges, peer experiences), considering origin (AI's training data), authority (does this suggestion serve their creative vision?), context (their specific project needs), using corroboration across rounds to guide selection of useful approaches.
D3.2.9-12
Evaluate the credibility of a source by examining how experts value the source.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Students evaluate AI suggestions' credibility by examining whether they ring true creatively (expert evaluation from their own creative judgment), distinguishing between pattern-based suggestions that feel generic vs. ideas that genuinely serve their authentic vision—learning to be "experts" in evaluating AI contributions.
D3.3.9-12
Identify evidence that draws information from multiple sources to support the validity of an argument or to detect inconsistencies in evidence in order to revise or strengthen claims.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Students identify evidence across four rounds of collaboration, comparing AI responses under different conditions to support or revise claims about what helps: "Structured prompts work better" (supported by comparing Round 1 vs. Round 2 results), "AI struggles with authenticity" (detected through inconsistencies between generic suggestions and authentic needs).
D3.4.9-12
Refine claims and counterclaims attending to precision, significance, and knowledge conveyed through the claim while pointing out the strengths and limitations of both.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Through reflection, students refine claims about AI collaboration: adding precision ("AI helps with brainstorming but not final decisions"), acknowledging limitations ("This worked for my character development, but poetry might be different"), recognizing strengths and weaknesses of their observations.

Dimension 4: Communicating Conclusions and Taking Informed Action

D4.1.9-12
Construct arguments using precise and knowledgeable claims, with evidence from multiple sources, while acknowledging counterclaims and evidentiary weaknesses.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Field journal reflections require constructing arguments about AI's creative capabilities using precise claims supported by specific evidence from experimentation: "AI helped by generating character trait options" (evidence: Round 1 outputs), while acknowledging counterclaims ("but suggestions felt generic until I added specificity").
D4.2.9-12
Construct explanations using sound reasoning, correct sequence, examples, and details with significant and pertinent information and data, while acknowledging the strengths and weaknesses of the explanation.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Documentation requires constructing explanations for observed patterns using sound reasoning ("AI produced generic suggestions because my prompt was vague," connecting to training data concepts), sequencing observations across rounds, providing specific examples, acknowledging explanation limits ("I only tested 4 rounds, so this might not be comprehensive").
D4.3.9-12
Present adaptations of arguments and explanations that feature evocative ideas and perspectives on issues and topics to reach a range of audiences and venues outside the classroom.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Students prepare insights that can be shared beyond the classroom: understanding what AI can and cannot do creatively is relevant to anyone using creative AI tools—writers, students, professionals. The investigation connects to real-world venues where these tools are deployed and debated.
D4.6.9-12
Use disciplinary and interdisciplinary lenses to understand the characteristics and causes of local, regional, and global problems; instances of such problems in multiple contexts; and challenges and opportunities faced by those trying to address these problems over time and place.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Students use technological, creative, economic, and ethical lenses to understand AI's impact on creative work: technological lens (how systems work and fail), creative lens (what makes authentic expression), economic lens (implications for creative industries), ethical lens (questions about authorship and originality)—understanding challenges creative AI presents and opportunities for responsible deployment.
D4.7.9-12
Assess options for individual and collective action to address local, regional, and global problems by engaging in self-reflection, strategy identification, and complex causal reasoning.
Lesson 2.6 Alignment: Through self-reflection on collaboration experiences, students assess individual actions (how to use creative AI responsibly, maintaining authorship) and collective concerns (how should creative industries adapt? what regulations might be needed?), engaging in complex reasoning about causes (training data determines capabilities) and solutions (better training, clearer disclosures, human oversight).
